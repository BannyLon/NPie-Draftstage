# Brand Information Intake Questionnaire

Before starting market research, use this questionnaire to verify you have sufficient context. If key information is missing, ask the user these questions **before** proceeding with the 6-pillar workflow.

Group your questions together into one message — don't ask them one at a time.

---

## Must-Have Information (Block execution if missing)

These are non-negotiable. You cannot produce a meaningful strategy without them.

### 1. Brand Basics
- What is the brand name?
- What product(s) or service(s) does the brand offer?
- What specific market category does the brand operate in? (Be specific — "organic skincare for sensitive skin" not just "beauty")

### 2. Business Stage & Context
- What stage is the business in? (Pre-launch / Early-stage / Growth / Mature / Repositioning)
- How long has the brand been operating?
- What is the approximate revenue range or scale? (This helps calibrate competitor selection and budget recommendations)

### 3. Target Geography
- What is the brand's primary market? (Specific country/region)
- Are there secondary markets being considered for expansion?

### 4. Target Audience (Initial Understanding)
- Who does the brand believe its ideal customer is? (Even a rough sketch helps)
- Are there any existing customer data or personas?

### 5. Business Objectives
- What is the brand trying to achieve in the next 6–12 months? (Launch, grow revenue, enter new market, reposition, etc.)
- Are there specific KPIs or targets already defined?

---

## Nice-to-Have Information (Improves quality but won't block execution)

Ask about these if the conversation allows. They enrich the analysis but aren't strictly required.

### 6. Current Marketing Activity
- What marketing channels is the brand currently using?
- What has worked well? What hasn't?
- What is the approximate marketing budget (monthly/annual)?

### 7. Brand Identity
- Does the brand have an established visual identity (logo, brand colors, style guide)?
- What tone/voice does the brand use? (Formal, casual, playful, authoritative, etc.)
- Is there an existing tagline or positioning statement?

### 8. Known Competitors
- Are there specific competitors the brand watches or considers threats?
- What does the brand believe differentiates it from competitors?

### 9. Constraints & Preferences
- Are there any channels or tactics the brand specifically wants to avoid?
- Are there regulatory or compliance considerations in the brand's industry?
- Are there resource constraints (small team, limited tech stack, etc.)?

---

## How to Use This Questionnaire

1. **Check what you already know.** Review the conversation history and any provided materials for answers to these questions.
2. **Identify gaps.** Focus on the "Must-Have" section first. If you're missing 2+ items from this section, you need to ask.
3. **Ask efficiently.** Compile all missing questions into a single, organized message. Explain why you need each piece of information so the user understands the value of providing it.
4. **Proceed when ready.** Once you have all must-have items (and ideally some nice-to-haves), begin the 6-pillar workflow.
