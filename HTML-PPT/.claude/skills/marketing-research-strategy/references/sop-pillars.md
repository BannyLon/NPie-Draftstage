# SOP 六支柱详细研究步骤

本文件包含每个支柱的完整研究步骤和输出规范。在执行对应支柱时加载本文件的相关章节。

## Pillar 1: Market Landscape & Sizing

### Objective
Understand the overall market the brand operates in — its size, trajectory, and the forces shaping it. This provides the strategic context for every recommendation that follows.

### Research Steps

1. **Define the market category.** Identify the specific market or sub-market the brand operates in. Be as precise as possible — "DTC home textiles" is more useful than "home goods." If the brand spans multiple categories, identify the primary one that drives the majority of revenue.

2. **Estimate market size and growth.** Research the total addressable market (TAM) and serviceable addressable market (SAM) in the brand's primary geography. Look for recent market reports, analyst estimates, or industry publications that provide revenue figures and projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) for the next 3–5 years.

3. **Identify macro trends.** Research 3–5 macro-level trends actively shaping this market. These could be shifts in consumer behavior, technology changes, regulatory developments, economic conditions, or cultural movements. For each trend, note whether it represents a tailwind (opportunity) or headwind (challenge) for the brand.

4. **Map the market structure.** Identify how the market is segmented — by price tier (budget, mid-range, premium, luxury), by channel (DTC, retail, marketplace, wholesale), by customer segment (demographics, use case, geography), or by product sub-category. Determine where the brand currently sits within this structure and where the growth opportunities are.

5. **Identify seasonal patterns and demand cycles.** Research whether the market has predictable demand fluctuations tied to seasons, holidays, events, or industry cycles. Note peak and off-peak periods as these directly affect campaign timing and budget allocation.

### Output Requirements
A concise market overview covering:
- Market category definition
- Estimated market size and trajectory (TAM/SAM, CAGR)
- 3–5 key macro trends with their implications for the brand
- Market structure mapping showing where the brand sits
- Notable seasonal or cyclical demand patterns

## Pillar 2: Competitor Analysis

### Objective
Understand who the brand competes against, how competitors position themselves, what messaging they use, how they execute their marketing efforts, and where the vulnerabilities and gaps are.

### Research Steps

1. **Identify the competitive set.** List 5–8 direct competitors (same product category, similar price tier, overlapping audience) and 2–3 indirect competitors (different product but same customer need). For each competitor, note their estimated size or revenue range if available.

2. **Analyze positioning and messaging.** For each direct competitor, review their homepage, about page, and primary product pages. Document:
   - Their tagline or core positioning statement
   - The primary benefit they lead with
   - The emotional angle they use (aspirational, practical, community-driven)
   - Any unique claims or differentiators they highlight

3. **Audit marketing channels.** For each direct competitor, research their presence and activity level across:
   - Organic search (what keywords they rank for, estimated traffic)
   - Paid search and shopping ads (whether they're bidding on brand and non-brand terms)
   - Social media (which platforms they're active on, posting frequency, follower count, engagement patterns)
   - Email marketing (sign up for their list, note frequency and content themes)
   - Content marketing (blog, video, podcast — topics and cadence)

4. **Evaluate customer experience.** Go through the purchase funnel for 2–3 top competitors. Note:
   - Website UX and navigation quality
   - Checkout process friction points
   - Shipping costs and speed
   - Return policy clarity
   - Post-purchase communication (confirmation emails, tracking updates, follow-up sequences)

5. **Identify competitive gaps and opportunities.** Synthesize findings to identify:
   - Underserved customer segments
   - Messaging angles no one is owning
   - Channels competitors are neglecting
   - Pricing tiers with white space
   - Product features or benefits being overlooked

### Output Requirements
- A competitor matrix comparing 5–8 key players across: positioning statement, primary messaging angle, top 3 marketing channels, estimated traffic/revenue tier, and noted weaknesses
- A summary of 3–5 actionable competitive gaps the brand can exploit

## Pillar 3: Target Audience Deep Dive

### Objective
Develop a nuanced understanding of the brand's ideal customer — not just demographics, but psychographics, behaviors, pain points, and decision-making triggers.

### Research Steps

1. **Define primary and secondary audiences.** Based on existing brand data (if available) or market assumptions, define 1–2 primary audience personas and 1 secondary persona. Include age, gender, location, income, occupation, family status.

2. **Uncover psychographics and motivations.** Research what drives this audience: values, lifestyle, aspirations, fears, hobbies, media consumption habits. Use tools like SparkToro, Reddit threads, Amazon reviews, or social listening to find authentic voice-of-customer insights.

3. **Map the customer journey.** Outline the typical path from awareness → consideration → purchase → loyalty for this audience. Identify key touchpoints, information sources, and decision criteria at each stage.

4. **Identify pain points and unmet needs.** What frustrations does this audience have with current solutions? What do they wish existed? What trade-offs are they forced to make?

5. **Validate with real-world data.** Where possible, supplement assumptions with survey data, interview quotes, or behavioral analytics (e.g., heatmaps, session recordings).

### Output Requirements
Detailed audience personas including:
- Demographic profile
- Psychographic drivers
- Top 3 pain points
- Preferred content formats/channels
- A mapped customer journey with key decision moments

## Pillar 4: Brand Positioning & Messaging Framework

### Objective
Craft a clear, differentiated positioning statement and messaging hierarchy that resonates with the target audience and leverages identified market opportunities.

### Research Steps

1. **Synthesize insights from Pillars 1–3.** Combine market landscape, competitor gaps, and audience insights to identify the brand's unique value proposition.

2. **Draft positioning statement.** Use the formula:
   > "For [target audience], [brand] is the [category] that delivers [key benefit] because [reason to believe]."

3. **Develop messaging hierarchy.** Create:
   - **Core brand promise** (1 sentence)
   - **Primary messaging pillar** (supporting the core promise)
   - **Secondary messaging pillars** (2–3 additional supporting points)
   - **Proof points** (data, testimonials, features that back up claims)

4. **Test for differentiation.** Compare draft messaging against competitor messaging — ensure it doesn't sound generic or easily replicable.

5. **Align with business goals.** Ensure messaging supports short-term conversion goals and long-term brand equity building.

### Output Requirements
- Finalized positioning statement
- Messaging hierarchy (core + primary + secondary pillars)
- 3–5 proof points ready for creative development

## Pillar 5: Channel Strategy & Tactical Plan

### Objective
Translate positioning into actionable channel-specific tactics that maximize reach, engagement, and conversion based on audience behavior and competitive landscape.

### Research Steps

1. **Prioritize channels.** Based on audience media consumption and competitor activity, select 3–5 priority channels (e.g., Instagram, Google Ads, Email, TikTok, Podcasts).

2. **Define channel objectives.** For each channel, specify:
   - Primary goal (awareness, consideration, conversion)
   - KPIs
   - Expected ROI timeline

3. **Develop tactical playbooks.** For each channel, outline:
   - Content types and formats
   - Posting frequency and cadence
   - Paid vs. organic mix
   - Key influencers or partners (if applicable)
   - Budget allocation estimate

4. **Integrate cross-channel synergy.** Ensure messaging and visuals are consistent across channels while adapting format to platform norms.

5. **Build measurement framework.** Define how success will be tracked per channel and overall campaign.

### Output Requirements
- Channel-by-channel tactical plan including: objective, KPIs, content strategy, budget split, and measurement approach
- Integrated campaign calendar for first 90 days

## Pillar 6: Risk Assessment & Contingency Planning

### Objective
Anticipate potential roadblocks, external threats, or execution risks — and prepare mitigation strategies to ensure strategy resilience.

### Research Steps

1. **Identify internal risks.** e.g., resource constraints, team bandwidth, tech limitations, approval bottlenecks.

2. **Identify external risks.** e.g., economic downturn, new regulations, competitor moves, supply chain disruptions.

3. **Assess likelihood and impact.** Rate each risk on probability (low/medium/high) and severity (low/medium/high).

4. **Develop contingency plans.** For high-probability/high-impact risks, create backup plans: alternative channels, messaging pivots, budget reallocation options.

5. **Establish monitoring triggers.** Define early warning signs that would activate contingency plans.

### Output Requirements
- Risk register with rated risks
- Mitigation strategies for each identified risk
- Trigger-based action plans
- Owner assignments and review cadence
